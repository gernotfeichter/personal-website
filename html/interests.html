Due to current events I thought a little bit about renewable energy and came to the conclusions listed below. This is not intended to be complete list of "all there is on the market", it is rather a compiled list of selected technologies that actually make sense for residential homes in terms of energy efficiency and cost efficiency according to my own research and conclusions.

Production

Note: I decided to use the payback period as the most important indicator to compare energy production systems below.

Solar

Solar Photovoltaic is a viable energy production technology and has a payback period of six to ten years [1].

Wind

Things are little bit more complicated with wind energy, it seems to be more economical in the large scale [5] than in the small scale at the moment [4]. It is my hope that this could change in future, see the following section on Betz's law below, that I am currently evaluating.

Betz's law

Graham Flowers posted a video on youtube [3] that claims that his wind turbine is more efficient than what the conventional model of Betz allows (more than 59% efficiency).

I consider it worthwhile to evaluate the proposed solution!
Right now I only checked the math and while I did not arrive at the exact same numbers as he, I arrived at a similar result.

Claim: Wind turbine with corrugated foils with a diameter of 2.9m produces 794 watts at a wind speed of 6.7m/s.

My calculation of Betz's limit for the given claim:

How much volume of air passes through the turbine in one second? 44m^3.
https://www.wolframalpha.com/input?i=volume+of+a+cylinder+with+2.9m+diameter+and+6.7m+hight

What is the mass of that quantity of air? 56.1kg
https://www.wolframalpha.com/input?i=44m%5E3+air

What is the kinetic energy of 56.1kg moving at 6.7m/s? 0.3498 watt hours
https://www.wolframalpha.com/input?i=energy+in+56.1kg+moving+at+6.7m%2Fs

What is that kinetic energy (previous answer) in watt seconds? 1259.3 watt seconds
https://www.wolframalpha.com/input?i=0.3498+watt+hours+to+watt+seconds

How much power should the turbine output maximally, after applying Betz's law? 746.25 watt seconds
https://www.wolframalpha.com/input?i=1259.3+*++%2816%2F27%29

Storage

The importance of a storage arises from the fluctuating nature of wind and solar. Unfortunately, there are no cost-effective energy storage solutions available at the moment [2]. Fortunately, there are many promising technologies on the rise, though to many to list.

Conclusions

It is currently not financially viable to become self-sustaining by going 100% renewable, but it is possible from a technical perspective! The current best bet is to go solar and possibly combine it with a heat pump to partially live on renewables. Advancements in storage solutions are required, advancements in energy production solutions could furthermore enhance economic viability.
